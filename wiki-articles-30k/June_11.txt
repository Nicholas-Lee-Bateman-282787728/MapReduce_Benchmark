      1184 BC &ndash; Trojan War: Troy is sacked and burned, according to calculations by Eratosthenes. 173 &ndash; Marcomannic Wars: The Roman army in Moravia is encircled by the Quadi, who have broken the peace treaty (171). In a violent thunderstorm emperor Marcus Aurelius defeats and subdues them in the so-called "miracle of the rain". 631 &ndash; Emperor Taizong of Tang, the Emperor of China, sends envoys to the Xueyantuo bearing gold and silk in order to seek the release of enslaved Chinese prisoners captured during the transition from Sui to Tang from the northern frontier; this embassy succeeded in freeing 80,000 Chinese men and women who were then returned to China. 786 &ndash; A Hasanid Alid uprising in Mecca is crushed by the Abbasids at the Battle of Fakhkh. Idris ibn Abdallah flees to the Maghreb, where he later founds the Idrisid dynasty. 1118 &ndash; Roger of Salerno, Prince of Antioch, captures Azaz from the Seljuk Turks. 1157 &ndash; Albert I of Brandenburg, also called, The Bear (Ger: Albrecht der Bär), becomes the founder of the Margraviate of Brandenburg, Germany and the first Margrave. 1345 &ndash; The megas doux Alexios Apokaukos, chief minister of the Byzantine Empire, is lynched by political prisoners. 1429 &ndash; Hundred Years' War: Start of the Battle of Jargeau. 1488 &ndash; Battle of Sauchieburn: Fought between rebel Lords and James III of Scotland, resulting in the death of the King. 1509 &ndash; Henry VIII of England marries Catherine of Aragon. 1594 &ndash; Philip II recognizes the rights and privileges of the local nobles and chieftains in the Philippines, which paved way to the stabilization of the rule of the Principalía (an elite ruling class of native nobility in Spanish Philippines). 1770 &ndash; British explorer Captain James Cook runs aground on the Great Barrier Reef. 1775 &ndash; The American Revolutionary War's first naval engagement, the Battle of Machias, results in the capture of a small British naval vessel. 1776 &ndash; The Continental Congress appoints Thomas Jefferson, John Adams, Benjamin Franklin, Roger Sherman, and Robert R. Livingston to the Committee of Five to draft a declaration of independence. 1788 &ndash; Russian explorer Gerasim Izmailov reaches Alaska. 1805 &ndash; A fire consumes large portions of Detroit in the Michigan Territory. 1825 &ndash; The first cornerstone is laid for Fort Hamilton in New York City. 1837 &ndash; The Broad Street Riot occurs in Boston, fueled by ethnic tensions between Yankees and Irish. 1865 &ndash; The Naval Battle of Riachuelo is fought on the rivulet Riachuelo (Argentina), between the Paraguayan Navy on one side and the Brazilian Navy on the other. The Brazilian victory was crucial for the later success of the Triple Alliance (Brazil, Uruguay and Argentina) in the Paraguayan War. 1892 &ndash; The Limelight Department, one of the world's first film studios, is officially established in Melbourne, Australia. 1895 &ndash; Paris–Bordeaux–Paris is sometimes called the first automobile race in history or the "first motor race". 1898 &ndash; Spanish–American War: U.S. war ships set sail for Cuba. 1898   &ndash; The Hundred Days' Reform is started by Guangxu Emperor with a plan to change social, political and educational institutions in China, but is suspended by Empress Dowager Cixi after 104 days. A group of Serbian officers stormed royal palace and assassinated King Alexander Obrenović and his wife queen Draga. 1907 &ndash; George Dennett, aided by Gilbert Jessop, dismisses Northamptonshire for 12 runs, the lowest total in first-class cricket. 1917 &ndash; King Alexander assumes the throne of Greece after his father Constantine I abdicates under pressure by allied armies occupying Athens. 1919 &ndash; Sir Barton wins the Belmont Stakes, becoming the first horse to win the U.S. Triple Crown. 1920 &ndash;
