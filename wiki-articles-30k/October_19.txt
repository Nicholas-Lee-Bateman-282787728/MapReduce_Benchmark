     202 BC &ndash; Second Punic War: At the Battle of Zama, Roman legions under Scipio Africanus defeat Hannibal Barca, leader of the army defending Carthage. 439 &ndash; The Vandals, led by King Gaiseric, take Carthage in North Africa. 1216 &ndash; King John of England dies at Newark-on-Trent and is succeeded by his nine-year-old son Henry. 1386 &ndash; The Universität Heidelberg held its first lecture, making it the oldest German university. 1466 &ndash; The Thirteen Years' War ends with the Second Treaty of Thorn. 1469 &ndash; Ferdinand II of Aragon marries Isabella I of Castile, a marriage that paves the way to the unification of Aragon and Castile into a single country, Spain. 1512 &ndash; Martin Luther becomes a doctor of theology (Doctor in Biblia). 1649 &ndash; New Ross town, County Wexford, Ireland, surrenders to Oliver Cromwell. 1781 &ndash; At Yorktown, Virginia, representatives of British commander Lord Cornwallis handed over Cornwallis' sword and formally surrendered to George Washington and the comte de Rochambeau. 1789 &ndash; Chief Justice John Jay is sworn in as the first Chief Justice of the United States. 1805 &ndash; Napoleonic Wars: Austrian General Mack surrenders his army to the Grande Armée of Napoleon Bonaparte at the Battle of Ulm.
