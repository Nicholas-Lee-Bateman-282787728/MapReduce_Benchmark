September 5&nbsp;– Wagnerian tenor Walter Widdop appears at The Proms, singing "Lohengrin's Farewell", the day before his sudden death at the age of 51."Mr. Walter Widdop – A Fine English Tenor", The Times, 7 September 1949, p. 7 December 15&nbsp;– Birdland jazz club opens in New York City. December 24&nbsp;– At the start of the Holy Year, Charles Gounod's Inno e Marcia Pontificale is adopted as the new papal anthem. December 29&nbsp;– Les Paul and Mary Ford marry. The Boccherini Quintet is formed in Rome. Ravi Shankar becomes music director of All India Radio. Mitch Miller begins his career as one of the 20th century's most successful record producers at Mercury Eddie Fisher is "discovered" by Eddie Cantor and signs with RCA. Bob Hope suggests that Anthony Benedetto change his stage name from "Joe Bari" to "Tony Bennett" Frankie Laine records "Mule Train", considered by some critics as marking the beginning of the rock era. Teresa Brewer makes her first recording on the London label. The Ames Brothers become the first artists to record for Coral Records, a subsidiary of Decca. Johnnie Ray performs at the Flame Showbar in Detroit. The legendary Al Jolson records the soundtrack to "Jolson Sings Again," the sequel to his hugely successful biopic "The Jolson Story" (1946) 45 rpm discs are introduced Gorni Kramer starts working for musical impresarios Garinei and Giovannini.
