The Treaty of Hopewell is signed. 1811 &ndash; Beethoven's Piano Concerto No. 5 in E-flat major, Op. 73, premieres at the Gewandhaus in Leipzig. 1814 &ndash; The Times in London is for the first time printed by automatic, steam-powered presses built by the German inventors Friedrich Koenig and Andreas Friedrich Bauer, signaling the beginning of the availability of newspapers to a mass audience. 1821 &ndash; Mayer opens his first movie theater. 1908 &ndash; A mine explosion in Marianna, Pennsylvania, kills 154 men, leaving only one survivor. 1909 &ndash; Sergei Rachmaninoff makes the debut performance of his Piano Concerto No. 3, considered to be one of the most technically challenging piano concertos in the standard classical repertoire. 1910 &ndash; The Liberal Party, led by Eleftherios Venizelos, wins the second Greek general election of the year. 1912 &ndash; Albania declares its independence from the Ottoman Empire. 1914 &ndash; World War I: Following a war-induced closure in July, the New York Stock Exchange re-opens for bond trading. 1917 &ndash; The Estonian Provincial Assembly declares itself the sovereign power of Estonia. 1918 &ndash; Bukovina votes for union with the Kingdom of Romania. 1919 &ndash; Lady Astor is elected as a Member of the Parliament of the United Kingdom.
