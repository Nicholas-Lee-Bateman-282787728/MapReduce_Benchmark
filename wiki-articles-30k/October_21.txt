Napoleonic Wars: Battle of Trafalgar: A British fleet led by Vice Admiral Lord Nelson defeats a combined French and Spanish fleet off the coast of Spain under Admiral Villeneuve, signaling almost the end of French maritime power and leaves Britain's navy unchallenged until the 20th century. 1816 &ndash; The Penang Free School is founded in George Town, Penang, Malaysia, by the Rev Hutchings, the oldest English-language school in Southeast Asia. 1824 &ndash; Joseph Aspdin patents Portland cement. 1854 &ndash; Florence Nightingale and a staff of 38 nurses are sent to the Crimean War. 1861 &ndash; American Civil War: Battle of Ball's Bluff: Union forces under Colonel Edward Baker are defeated by Confederate troops in the second major battle of the war. Baker, a close friend of Abraham Lincoln, is killed in the fighting. 1867 &ndash; Manifest destiny: Medicine Lodge Treaty: Near Medicine Lodge, Kansas a landmark treaty is signed by southern Great Plains Indian leaders. The treaty requires Native American Plains tribes to relocate to a reservation in western Oklahoma. 1879 &ndash; Thomas Edison invents a workable electric light bulb at his laboratory in Menlo Park, N.J. which was tested the next day and lasted 13.5 hours. This would be the invention of the first commercially practical incandescent light. Popular belief is that he invented the first light bulb, which he did not. 1888 &ndash; Foundation of the Swiss Social Democratic Party. 1892 &ndash; Opening ceremonies for the World's Columbian Exposition are held in Chicago, though because construction was behind schedule, the exposition did not open until May 1, 1893. 1895 &ndash; The Republic of Formosa collapses as Japanese forces invade. 1902 &ndash; In the United States, a five-month strike by United Mine Workers ends. 1910 &ndash;  arrives in Halifax Harbour to become the first ship of the Royal Canadian Navy. 1912 &ndash; First Balkan War: Kardzhali is liberated by Bulgarian forces. 1921 &ndash; President Warren G.
