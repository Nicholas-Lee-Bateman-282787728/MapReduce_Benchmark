                                   January 1 &ndash; First week as No. 1 single on Billboard and Cashbox charts in the United States of Patti Page hit song "Tennessee Waltz". January 4 &ndash; Korean War: Third Battle of Seoul: Chinese and North Korean forces capture Seoul for the second time (they had lost Seoul in the Second Battle of Seoul in September 1950). January 9 &ndash; The Government of the United Kingdom announces abandonment of the Tanganyika groundnut scheme for the cultivation of peanuts in the Tanganyika Territory with the writing off of £36.5M debt. January 15 &ndash; In a court in West Germany, Ilse Koch, The "Witch of Buchenwald", wife of the commandant of the Buchenwald concentration camp, is sentenced to life imprisonment. January 20 &ndash; Avalanches in the Alps kill 240 and bury 45,000 for a time in Switzerland, Austria and Italy. January 25 &ndash; Dutch author Anne de Vries releases the first volume of her novel Journey Through the Night (Reis door de nacht) set during World War II. January 27 &ndash; Nuclear testing at the Nevada Test Site begins with a 1-kiloton bomb dropped on Frenchman Flat, northwest of Las Vegas. February &ndash; Convention People's Party wins national elections in Gold Coast (British colony). February 1 &ndash; The United Nations General Assembly declares that China is an aggressor in the Korean War in United Nations General Assembly Resolution 498. February 4&ndash;February 8 &ndash; Surgeons remove an ovarian cyst from Gertrude Levandowski in a 96-hour long operation in Chicago. She loses almost half of her weight and emerges weighing 140&nbsp;kg. February 6 &ndash; A Pennsylvania Railroad passenger train derails near Woodbridge Township, New Jersey, killing 85 people and injuring over 500, in one of the worst rail disasters in American history. February 12 &ndash; Muhammad Reza Shah marries Soraya Esfandiary-Bakhtiari. February 15 &ndash; Start of the 1951 New Zealand waterfront dispute, which lasts for 151 days. February 19 &ndash; Jean Lee becomes the last woman hanged in Australia, when Lee and her 2 pimps are hanged for the murder and torture of a 73-year-old bookmaker. February 27 &ndash; The Twenty-second Amendment to the United States Constitution, limiting Presidents to two terms, is ratified. March 2 &ndash; The first NBA All-Star game is played in the Boston Garden. March 3 or 5 &ndash; Jackie Brenston and His Delta Cats record "Rocket 88" in Memphis, Tennessee, often considered the first rock and roll song on record (released in April). March 6 &ndash; The trial of Julius and Ethel Rosenberg for conspiracy to commit espionage begins in the United States. March 9 &ndash; United Artists releases sci-fi film The Man from Planet X. March 12 &ndash; Hank Ketcham's best-selling comic strip Dennis the Menace appeared in newspapers across the United States for the first time. March 14 Korean War: For the second time, United Nations troops recapture Seoul during Operation Ripper. West Germany joins UNESCO. March 29 Second Red Scare: Julius and Ethel Rosenberg are convicted of conspiracy to commit espionage. On April 5 they are sentenced to receive the death penalty. Rodgers and Hammerstein's The King and I opens on Broadway and runs for three years. It is the first Rodgers & Hammerstein musical specifically written for an actress (Gertrude Lawrence). Lawrence is stricken with cancer during the run of the show and dies halfway through its run a year later. The show makes a star of Yul Brynner. The 23rd Academy Awards ceremony is held; All About Eve wins the Best Picture award and four others. March 31 &ndash; Remington Rand delivers the first UNIVAC I computer to the United States Census Bureau. April 5&ndash;13 &ndash; The most complete recording of George Gershwin's opera Porgy and Bess up to this date, a 3-LP Columbia Masterworks Records 129-minute album in mono, is made; it is released to great critical acclaim. April 1 &ndash; Female suffrage begins in Greece. April 11 U.S. President Harry S. Truman relieves General Douglas MacArthur of his Far Eastern commands. After its removal from Westminster Abbey on Christmas Day, 1950, the Stone of Scone resurfaces on the altar of Arbroath Abbey. April 18 &ndash; The Treaty of Paris (1951) is adopted, establishing the European Coal and Steel Community. April 21 &ndash; The National Olympic Committee of the Soviet Union is formed. The USSR first participates in the Olympic Games at Helsinki, Finland, in 1952. April 24 &ndash; In Yokohama, Japan, a fire on a train kills more than 100. April 28 &ndash; Robert Menzies' Liberal Party government in Australia is re-elected for a second term. April 29 &ndash; RKO releases the Howard Hawks sci-fi film, The Thing (From Another World). May 1 &ndash; The opera house of Geneva, Switzerland is almost destroyed in a fire. May 3 King George VI opens London's Royal Festival Hall as a patron. The Festival of Britain opens. The U.S. Senate Committee on Armed Services and U.S. Senate Committee on Foreign Relations begins its closed door hearings into the dismissal of General Douglas MacArthur by U.S. President Harry S Truman. May 9 &ndash; Operation Greenhouse: The first thermonuclear weapon is tested on Enewetok Atoll in the Marshall Islands, by the United States. May 14 &ndash; The first volunteer-run passenger trains run on Talyllyn Railway, Wales. May 15 &ndash; A military coup occurs in Bolivia. May 21 &ndash; The 9th Street Art Exhibition, otherwise known as the Ninth Street Show, a gathering of a number of notable artists, marks the stepping-out of the post war New York avant-garde, collectively known as the New York School. May 23 &ndash; The Tibetan government signs the Seventeen Point Agreement for the Peaceful Liberation of Tibet with the People's Republic of China. May 25 &ndash; The first atomic bomb "boosted" by the inclusion of thermonuclear materials, is tested in the "Item" test on Enewetok Atoll in the Marshall Islands by the United States. May 28 &ndash; The Goon Show is first broadcast on BBC Home Service in the U.K.; the first series was entitled "Crazy People". June 4 &ndash; Foley Square trial concludes review in U.S. Supreme Court as Dennis v. United States, with a ruling against the defendants (overturned by Yates v. United States in 1957) June 14 &ndash; UNIVAC I is dedicated by the U.S. Census Bureau. MGM's Technicolor film version of Show Boat, starring Kathryn Grayson, Ava Gardner and Howard Keel, premieres at Radio City Music Hall in New York City. July 14 &ndash; In Joplin, Missouri, the George Washington Carver National Monument becomes the first United States National Monument to honor an African American. July 16 &ndash; King Léopold III of Belgium abdicates in favour of his son Baudouin. July 17 &ndash; King Baudouin takes the oath as king of Belgium. July 20 &ndash; King Abdullah I of Jordan is assassinated by a Palestinian while attending Friday prayers in Jerusalem. He is succeeded by his son, King Talal. July 26 &ndash; Walt Disney's 13th animated film, Alice in Wonderland, premieres in London, United Kingdom.
