(The Palmer Peninsula is later named after him.) 1831 &ndash; Ecuador and Venezuela are separated from Gran Colombia. 1839 &ndash; Oberto, Giuseppe Verdi's first opera, opens at the Teatro alla Scala in Milan, Italy. 1856 &ndash; American Old West: On the Sonoita River in present-day southern Arizona, the United States Army establishes Fort Buchanan in order to help control new land acquired in the Gadsden Purchase. 1858 &ndash; Modified Julian Day zero. 1863 &ndash; American Civil War: Siege of Knoxville begins: Confederate forces led by General James Longstreet place Knoxville, Tennessee, under siege. 1869 &ndash; In Egypt, the Suez Canal, linking the Mediterranean Sea with the Red Sea, is inaugurated. 1871 &ndash; The National Rifle Association is granted a charter by the state of New York. 1876 &ndash; Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky's "Slavonic March" is given its premiere performance in Moscow, Russia. 1878 &ndash; First assassination attempt against Umberto I of Italy by anarchist Giovanni Passannante, who was armed with a dagger. The King survived with a slight wound in an arm. Prime Minister Benedetto Cairoli blocked the aggressor, receiving an injury in a leg. 1885 &ndash; Serbo-Bulgarian War: The decisive Battle of Slivnitsa begins. 1896 &ndash; The Western Pennsylvania Hockey League, which later became the first ice hockey league to openly trade and hire players, began play at Pittsburgh's Schenley Park Casino. 1903 &ndash; The Russian Social Democratic Labour Party splits into two groups: The Bolsheviks (Russian for "majority") and Mensheviks (Russian for "minority"). 1911 &ndash; Omega Psi Phi Fraternity Incorporated, which is the first black Greek-lettered organization founded at an American historically black college or university, was founded on the campus of Howard University in Washington, D.C. 1922 &ndash; Former Ottoman sultan Mehmed VI goes into exile in Italy. 1933 &ndash; United States recognizes Soviet Union. 1939 &ndash; Nine Czech students are executed as a response to anti-Nazi demonstrations prompted by the death of Jan Opletal. In addition, all Czech universities are shut down and over 1200 Czech students sent to concentration camps.
