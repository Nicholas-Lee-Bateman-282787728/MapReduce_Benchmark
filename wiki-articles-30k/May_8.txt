     453 BC &ndash; Spring and Autumn period: The house of Zhao defeats the house of Zhi, ending the Battle of Jinyang, a military conflict between the elite families of the State of Jin. 413 &ndash; Emperor Honorius signs an edict providing tax relief for the Italian provinces Tuscia, Campania, Picenum, Samnium, Apulia, Lucania and Calabria, which were plundered by the Visigoths. 589 &ndash; Reccared I summons the Third Council of Toledo. 1429 &ndash; Hernando de Soto reaches the Mississippi River and names  it Río de Espíritu Santo. 1788 &ndash; The French Parlement is suspended to be replaced by the creation of forty-seven new courts. 1794 &ndash; Branded a traitor during the Reign of Terror by revolutionists, French chemist Antoine Lavoisier, who was also a tax collector with the Ferme Générale, is tried, convicted, and guillotined all on the same day in Paris. 1821 &ndash; Greek War of Independence: The Greeks defeat the Turks at the Battle of Gravia Inn. 1842 &ndash; A train derails and catches fire in Paris, killing between 52 and 200 people. 1846 &ndash; Mexican–American War: The Battle of Palo Alto: Zachary Taylor defeats a Mexican force north of the Rio Grande in the first major battle of the war. 1861 &ndash; American Civil War: Richmond, Virginia is named the capital of the Confederate States of America. 1877 &ndash; At Gilmore's Gardens in New York City, the first Westminster Kennel Club Dog Show opens. 1886 &ndash; Pharmacist John Pemberton first sells a carbonated beverage named "Coca-Cola" as a patent medicine. 1898 &ndash; The first games of the Italian football league system are played. 1899 &ndash; The Irish Literary Theatre in Dublin produced its first play. 1901 &ndash; The Australian Labour Party is established. 1902 &ndash; In Martinique, Mount Pelée erupts, destroying the town of Saint-Pierre and killing over 30,000 people. Only a handful of residents survive the blast. 1912 &ndash; Paramount Pictures is founded. 1919 &ndash; Edward George Honey proposes the idea of a moment of silence to commemorate the Armistice of World War I. 1924 &ndash; The Klaipėda Convention is signed formally incorporating Klaipėda Region (Memel Territory) into Lithuania. 1927 &ndash; Attempting to make the first non-stop transatlantic flight from Paris to New York, French war heroes Charles Nungesser and François Coli disappear after taking off aboard The White Bird biplane. 1933 &ndash; Mohandas Gandhi begins a 21-day fast of self-purification and launched a one-year campaign to help the Harijan movement. 1941 &ndash; The German Luftwaffe launches a bombing raid on Nottingham and Derby 1942 &ndash; World War II: The Battle of the Kerch Peninsula: The German 11th Army begins Operation Trappenjagd (Bustard Hunt) and destroys the bridgehead of the three Soviet Armies (44th, 47th, and 51st) defending the Kerch Peninsula, in the eastern part of the Crimea. 1942   &ndash; World War II: The Battle of the Coral Sea comes to an end with Imperial Japanese Navy aircraft carrier aircraft attacking and sinking the United States Navy aircraft carrier . The battle marks the first time in the naval history that two enemy fleets fight without visual contact between warring ships. 1942   &ndash; World War II: Gunners of the Ceylon Garrison Artillery on Horsburgh Island in the Cocos Islands rebel in the Cocos Islands Mutiny. Their mutiny is crushed and three of them are executed, the only British Commonwealth soldiers to be executed for mutiny during the Second World War. 1945 &ndash; Hundreds of Algerian civilians are killed by French Army soldiers in the Sétif massacre. 1945   &ndash; World War II: V-E Day, combat ends in Europe. Alessandroni, his wife, and other state officials. 1967 &ndash; The Philippine province of Davao is split into three: Davao del Norte, Davao del Sur, and Davao Oriental. 1970 &ndash; The Hard Hat Riot occurs in the Wall Street area of New York City as blue-collar construction workers clash with demonstrators protesting the Vietnam War. 1972 &ndash; Vietnam War: U.S. President Richard Nixon announces his order to place mines in major North Vietnamese ports in order to stem the flow of weapons and other goods to that nation. 1972   &ndash; Four Black September terrorists hijack Sabena Flight 571.
