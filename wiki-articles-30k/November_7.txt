     This day marks the approximate midpoint of autumn in the Northern Hemisphere and of spring in the Southern Hemisphere (starting the season at the September equinox). 335 &ndash; Athanasius is banished to Trier, on charge that he prevented a grain fleet from sailing to Constantinople. 680 &ndash; The Sixth Ecumenical Council commences in Constantinople. 1426 &ndash; Lam Sơn uprising: Lam Sơn rebels emerge victorious against the Ming army in the Battle of Tốt Động – Chúc Động taking place in Đông Quan, in now Hanoi. 1492 &ndash; The Ensisheim meteorite, the oldest meteorite with a known date of impact, strikes the Earth around noon in a wheat field outside the village of Ensisheim, Alsace, France. 1619 &ndash; Elizabeth Stuart is crowned Queen of Bohemia. 1665 &ndash; The London Gazette, the oldest surviving journal, is first published. 1775 &ndash; John Murray, the Royal Governor of the Colony of Virginia, starts the first mass emancipation of slaves in North America by issuing Lord Dunmore's Offer of Emancipation, which offers freedom to slaves who abandoned their colonial masters in order to fight with Murray and the British. 1786 &ndash; The oldest musical organization in the United States is founded as the Stoughton Musical Society. 1811 &ndash; Tecumseh's War: The Battle of Tippecanoe is fought near present-day Battle Ground, Indiana, United States. 1837 &ndash; In Alton, Illinois, abolitionist printer Elijah P. Lovejoy is shot dead by a mob while attempting to protect his printing shop from being destroyed a third time. 1861 &ndash; American Civil War: Battle of Belmont: In Belmont, Missouri, Union forces led by General Ulysses S. Grant overrun a Confederate camp but are forced to retreat when Confederate reinforcements arrive. 1874 &ndash; A cartoon by Thomas Nast in Harper's Weekly, is considered the first important use of an elephant as a symbol for the United States Republican Party. 1885 &ndash; The completion of Canada's first transcontinental railway is symbolized by the Last Spike ceremony at Craigellachie, British Columbia. 1893 &ndash; Women's suffrage: Women in the U.S. state of Colorado are granted the right to vote, the second state to do so. 1900 &ndash; Battle of Leliefontein, a battle during which the Royal Canadian Dragoons win three Victoria Crosses. 1900   &ndash; The People's Party is founded in Cuba. 1907 &ndash; Delta Sigma Pi is founded at New York University. 1907   &ndash; Jesús García saves the entire town of Nacozari de García by driving a burning train full of dynamite six kilometers (3.7 miles) away before it can explode. 1908 &ndash; Butch Cassidy and the Sundance Kid are reportedly killed in San Vicente, Bolivia. 1910 &ndash; The first air freight shipment (from Dayton, Ohio, to Columbus, Ohio) is undertaken by the Wright brothers and department store owner Max Moorehouse. 1912 &ndash; The Deutsche Opernhaus (now Deutsche Oper Berlin) opens in the Berlin neighborhood of Charlottenburg, with a production of Beethoven's Fidelio. 1913 &ndash; The first day of the Great Lakes Storm of 1913, a massive blizzard that ultimately killed 250 and caused over $5 million (about $118,098,000 in 2013 dollars) damage. Winds reach hurricane force on this date. 1914 &ndash; The first issue of The New Republic magazine is published. 1914   &ndash; The German colony of Kiaochow Bay and its centre at Tsingtao are captured by Japanese forces. 1916 &ndash; Jeannette Rankin is the first woman elected to the United States Congress. 1917 &ndash; The Gregorian calendar date of the October Revolution, which gets its name from the Julian calendar date of 25 October. On this date in 1917, the Bolsheviks storm the Winter Palace. 1917   &ndash; World War I: Third Battle of Gaza ends: British forces capture Gaza from the Ottoman Empire. 1918 &ndash; The 1918 influenza epidemic spreads to Western Samoa, killing 7,542 (about 20% of the population) by the end of the year. 1918   &ndash; Kurt Eisner overthrows the Wittelsbach dynasty in the Kingdom of Bavaria. 1919 &ndash; The first Palmer Raid is conducted on the second anniversary of the Russian Revolution. La Guardia is elected the 99th mayor of New York City. 1940 &ndash; In Tacoma, Washington, the original Tacoma Narrows Bridge collapses in a windstorm, a mere four months after the bridge's completion. 1941 &ndash; World War II: Soviet hospital ship Armenia is sunk by German planes while evacuating refugees and wounded military and staff of several Crimean hospitals. It is estimated that over 5,000 people died in the sinking. 1944 &ndash; A passenger train derails in Aguadilla, Puerto Rico from excessive speed when descending a hill. Nixon's veto of the War Powers Resolution, which limits presidential power to wage war without congressional approval. 1975 &ndash; In Bangladesh, a joint force of people and soldiers takes part in an uprising led by Colonel Abu Taher that ousts and kills Brigadier Khaled Mosharraf, freeing the then house-arrested army chief and future president Maj-Gen.
