     495 BC &ndash; A newly constructed temple in honour of the god Mercury was dedicated in ancient Rome on the Circus Maximus, between the Aventine and Palatine hills.  To spite the senate and the consuls, the people awarded the dedication to a senior military officer, Marcus Laetorius. 392 &ndash; Emperor Valentinian II is assassinated while advancing into Gaul against the Frankish usurper Arbogast. He is found hanging in his residence at Vienne. 589 &ndash; King Authari marries Theodelinda, daughter of the Bavarian duke Garibald I. A Catholic, she has great influence among the Lombard nobility. 1252 &ndash; Pope Innocent IV issues the papal bull ad extirpanda, which authorizes, but also limits, the torture of heretics in the Medieval Inquisition. 1525 &ndash; Insurgent peasants led by Anabaptist pastor Thomas Müntzer were defeated at the Battle of Frankenhausen, ending the German Peasants' War in the Holy Roman Empire. 1536 &ndash; Anne Boleyn, Queen of England, stands trial in London on  charges of treason, adultery and incest. She is condemned to death by a specially-selected jury. 1567 &ndash; Mary, Queen of Scots marries James Hepburn, 4th Earl of Bothwell, her third husband. 1602 &ndash; Bartholomew Gosnold becomes the first recorded European to see Cape Cod. 1618 &ndash; Johannes Kepler confirms his previously rejected discovery of the third law of planetary motion (he first discovered it on March 8 but soon rejected the idea after some initial calculations were made). 1648 &ndash; The Treaty of Westphalia is signed. 1701 &ndash; The War of the Spanish Succession begins. 1718 &ndash; James Puckle, a London lawyer, patents the world's first machine gun. 1730 &ndash; Hugh Walpole effectively became the first Prime Minister of the United Kingdom. 1755 &ndash; Laredo, Texas is established by the Spaniards. 1776 &ndash; American Revolution: The Virginia Convention instructs its Continental Congress delegation to propose a resolution of independence from Great Britain, paving the way for the United States Declaration of Independence. 1791 &ndash; French Revolution: Maximilien Robespierre proposes the Self-denying Ordinance. 1792 &ndash; War of the First Coalition: France declares war on Kingdom of Sardinia. 1793 &ndash; Diego Marín Aguilera flies a glider for "about 360 meters", at a height of 5–6 meters, during one of the first attempted manned flights. 1796 &ndash; War of the First Coalition: Napoleon enters Milan in triumph. 1800 &ndash; King George III of the United Kingdom survives an assassination attempt by James Hadfield, who is later acquitted by reason of insanity. 1811 &ndash; Paraguay declares independence from Spain. 1817 &ndash; Opening of the first private mental health hospital in the United States, the Asylum for the Relief of Persons Deprived of the Use of Their Reason (now Friends Hospital) in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. 1836 &ndash; Francis Baily observes "Baily's beads" during an annular eclipse. 1849 &ndash; Troops of the Two Sicilies take Palermo and crush the republican government of Sicily. 1850 &ndash; The Bloody Island Massacre takes place in Lake County, California, in which a large number of Pomo Indians in Lake County are slaughtered by a regiment of the United States Cavalry, led by Nathaniel Lyon. 1851 &ndash; The first Australian gold rush is proclaimed, although the discovery had been made three months earlier. 1858 &ndash; Opening of the present Royal Opera House in Covent Garden, London. 1862 &ndash; President Abraham Lincoln signs a bill into law creating the United States Bureau of Agriculture. It is later renamed the United States Department of Agriculture. 1864 &ndash; American Civil War: Battle of Resaca, Georgia ends. 1864   &ndash; American Civil War: Battle of New Market, Virginia: Students from the Virginia Military Institute fight alongside the Confederate Army to force Union General Franz Sigel out of the Shenandoah Valley. 1869 &ndash; Women's suffrage: In New York, Susan B. Anthony and Elizabeth Cady Stanton form the National Woman Suffrage Association. 1891 &ndash; Pope Leo XIII defends workers' rights and property rights in the encyclical Rerum novarum, the beginning of modern Catholic social teaching. 1904 &ndash; Russo-Japanese War: The Russian minelayer Amur lays a minefield about 15 miles off Port Arthur and sinks Japan's battleships Hatsuse, 15,000 tons, with 496 crew and Yashima. 1905 &ndash; Las Vegas, is founded when , in what later would become downtown, are auctioned off. 1911 &ndash; In Standard Oil Co. of New Jersey v. Madero's brother Emilio Madero take the city of Torreón from the Federales. 1919 &ndash; The Winnipeg General Strike begins. By 11:00, almost the whole working population of Winnipeg had walked off the job. 1919   &ndash; Greek invasion of Smyrna. During the invasion, the Greek army kills or wounds 350 Turks. Those responsible are punished by the Greek Commander Aristides Stergiades. 1925 &ndash; Al-Insaniyyah, the first Arabic communist newspaper, is founded. 1928 &ndash; Walt Disney character Mickey Mouse premieres in his first cartoon, Plane Crazy. 1929 &ndash; A fire at the Cleveland Clinic in Cleveland, Ohio kills 123. 1932 &ndash; In an attempted coup d'état, the Prime Minister of Japan Inukai Tsuyoshi is assassinated. 1934 &ndash; Kārlis Ulmanis establishes an authoritarian government in Latvia. 1935 &ndash; The Moscow Metro is opened to the public. 1940 &ndash;  is recommissioned. Hoisington the first female United States Army Generals. 1970   &ndash; Philip Lafayette Gibbs and James Earl Green are killed at Jackson State University by police during student protests. 1972 &ndash; Okinawa, under U.S. military governance since its conquest in 1945, reverts to Japanese control. 1972   &ndash; In Laurel, Maryland, Arthur Bremer shoots and paralyzes Alabama Governor George Wallace while he is campaigning to become President. 1974 &ndash; Ma'alot massacre: Members of the Democratic Front for the Liberation of Palestine attack and take hostages at an Israeli school; a total of 31 people are killed, including 22 schoolchildren. 1986 &ndash; Elio de Angelis, was killed while testing the Brabham BT55 at the Paul Ricard circuit at Le Castellet. 1987 &ndash; The Soviet Union launches the Polyus prototype orbital weapons platform.
